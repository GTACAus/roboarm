Spider - Arachnida

Spiders have 7 joints for each leg. Spiders extend their legs out using hydraulic pressure. They do this by forcing blood (hemolymph) into them. If a spider loses too much blood then it no longer has enough internal pressure to fully push its legs out.

Spider legs are covered with hundreds of tiny hairs connected to the nervous system. They use these hairs to sense and interact with their environment.
